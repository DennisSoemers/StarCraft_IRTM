<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Base_Features">Base Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Terrain_Features&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Base Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=File:MapFeatures_LT.png&amp;filetimestamp=20090212154411&amp;" class="image"><img alt="" src="/starcraft/images/thumb/1/15/MapFeatures_LT.png/300px-MapFeatures_LT.png" width="300" height="542" srcset="/starcraft/images/1/15/MapFeatures_LT.png 1.5x, /starcraft/images/1/15/MapFeatures_LT.png 2x" /></a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_Expansion">Natural Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Terrain_Features&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Natural Expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some of the features that define your natural expansion:
</p>
<ul>
<li> closest expansion to your main base.
</li>
<li> on most maps the natural expansion leads directly to the main base.
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Choke">Choke</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Terrain_Features&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Choke">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A choke is a narrow pathway or area that creates a funneling effect when moving through it. Similar to high ground, a choke massively favors the defender over the attacker. Examples of a choke include ramps and narrow passageways. If someone refers to 'the choke', they usually mean the narrowed entrance to the natural, as the choke separating the natural and main base is usually just called the 'ramp'. Chokes are very important tactically because they can be used to maximize the efficacy of your units while minimizing the opponent's.
</p><p>For example, blocking a ramp with two <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> against <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> is very powerful because the Zerglings, although numerically superior, will not be able to surround the Zealots and will be forced to fight them in small numbers. Only two or three Zerglings will be attacking the Zealots at any given time, while all the other Zerglings will not be doing anything useful. 
</p><p>The same principle can be applied using mineral patches; position your <a href="/starcraft/Marine" title="Marine">Marines</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Firebat" title="Firebat">Firebats</a> or even Zealots behind the opponent's mineral patches, as they will be very hard to surround. All other things being equal, this will ensure your units inflict the maximum damage possible and will require your opponent to actively micro against your units in order to kill them.
</p><p>Ranged units gain tremendously from using chokes. For example, in large <a href="/starcraft/Dragoon" title="Dragoon">Dragoon</a> vs. Dragoon battles, the side that has more space will have an advantage because they will have a greater number of ranged units able to attack simultaneously compared to their opponent. Picture a funnel with the narrow side being filled by one Dragoon army and the wide side by another. The Dragoons on the wide side can attack all at once, while the other army only has a very low number of attacking Dragoons at any given time. This advantage means that a smaller Dragoon army can defeat a larger one if it has the wide side of the choke. Be aware of this advantage and in ranged vs. ranged battles try to lure the opponent into a choke and maximize the number of units you have attacking at any one time. If caught in a choke, it is often useful to "step forward" with your units, to ensure all of them are within attacking range and therefore dealing damage.
</p><p>In ZvT, Zergs often secure a ramp by positioning two Lurkers on high ground. In early or early-mid game these are difficult to remove without suffering great losses because the Terran lacks space to spread his Marines. If you take into account the advantage high ground gives on top of this, it is clear why Terrans avoid directly confronting Lurkers that are defending a ramp.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mineral_Only">Mineral Only</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Terrain_Features&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mineral Only">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Any expansion on the map consisting of only minerals and no vespene gas.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Features">General Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Terrain_Features&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: General Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="High_Ground">High Ground</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Terrain_Features&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: High Ground">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>High ground is a relative attribution. When a unit is on a terrain level higher than its opposing unit, it is on the high ground while the other unit is on the low ground. Most maps only have two heights of terrain. Being on the high ground yields several advantages:
</p>
<ul>
<li> The unit won't be seen. When the high-ground unit fires, it will become visible for a short time.
</li>
<li> Units firing from the low ground have a ~47% chance to <a href="/starcraft/Damage#Misses" title="Damage">miss</a> their shots entirely.
</li>
</ul>
