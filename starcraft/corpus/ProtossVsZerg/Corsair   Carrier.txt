<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Corsair/Carrier_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term "<b>Corsair/Carrier</b>" describes a unit mix rather than a fixed Build Order. Usually, Corsairs and Carriers are used in the later stages of Protoss vs. Zerg. The switch to Carriers is usually done if either a <a href="/starcraft/Corsair/Reaver_(vs._Zerg)" title="Corsair/Reaver (vs. Zerg)">Corsair/Reaver</a> or <a href="/starcraft/Corsair/Dark_Templar_(vs._Zerg)" title="Corsair/Dark Templar (vs. Zerg)">Corsair/Dark Templar</a> strategy was used earlier. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order">Build Order</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Corsair/Carrier_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Build Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The opening for a Corsair/Carrier mix usually is a <a href="/starcraft/Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)" title="Protoss FE (vs. Zerg)">standard Forge first FE</a>. After that only requirements can be given.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution">Execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Corsair/Carrier_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Going for a Corsair/Carrier mix right after fast expanding will usually be countered by almost any Zerg opening. Thus, using a Corsair heavy strategy before teching to a Fleet Beacon and Carriers is a requirement. Usually, Carriers are only useful in the late stages of a game. Protoss needs relatively much Vespene Gas to build the Carriers in the first place. 
</p><p>Switching to Carriers makes sense the most in scenarios in which the Zerg uses Ultralisks and Zerglings to control the map. The more Hydralisks or Spire unit Zerg controls, the less effective a Carrier switch will be. Therefore Protoss will try to disguise the switch to Carriers as long as possible. 
</p><p>The transition itself should be prepared with care, as both Corsairs and Carriers use supply, which cannot be used to reinforce bases by Protoss. Constantly harassing with Dark Templars and Reavers is consequently one of the best options to buy time for the Carriers to built. Large scaled attacks on Expansions or massive drops are more careful in this stage for Protoss. 
</p><p>The Carriers should be moved carefully. Larger amounts of Devourers might render their attack useless for a few minutes. To counter larger groups of Devourers and Mutalisks, Shuttles with High Templar or Dark Archons can be used as support. Psionic Storm and Maelstrom counter Zerg's response to the air fleet. 
</p><p>The prime target of Carriers are the Hatcheries at mining expansions. These should be focused and the fleet should move back once the building was destroyed. Spore Colonies and Hydralisks can be neutralized with the Disruption Web spell of Corsairs. 
</p><p>Furthermore an Observer should be moved with the army at any point of time. Burrowed Hydralisks can be scouted in time. Furthermore, Defilers should be sniped if possible, as their Plague spell poses a greater risk for carriers.
</p><p><br />
</p>
