<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fast expand is a macro-oriented opening build designed to give Protoss an economic edge so as to establish and retain map control in the <a href="/starcraft/Mid_game" title="Mid game">mid game</a> by sacrificing it in the <a href="/starcraft/Early_game" title="Early game">early game</a>. Most of the transitions from this opening requires heavy gas usage, so it is mainly feasible on maps with a Gas Natural Expansion.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order">Build Order</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Build Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Fast Expansion can be done by either expanding with Photon Cannons and a Forge, or by building a Gateway first and delaying the expansion a bit. 
</p>

<dl>
<dt>Execution
</dt>
</dl>
<p>The Fast Expansion build order is the most common opening in the Protoss vs. Zerg Match Up. The information obtained by the first Probe at eight supply is crucial. Protoss has to do a trade off between being safe against aggressive openings in the early game and boosting his economy as fast as possible. Depending on the gathered intel, the Natural Buildings (Nexus, Forge, Gateway and Photon Cannons) are placed down by the logic described in the Build Order table above. It is utmost important to position the buildings in such a way that Zergling runbys are denied. Most maps are covered in the <a href="/starcraft/Protoss_Fast_Expand_Forge_Walling" title="Protoss Fast Expand Forge Walling">Protoss Fast Expand Forge Walling</a> article. 
</p><p>The number of Cannons needed to be safe from early aggression depends on several factors, such as the number of Zerglings built by Zerg, whether or not Speedupgrade for Zerglings is upgraded early or delayed and a possible early Hydralisk Den. A general rule of thumb says that as much Cannons should be build to be safe, but as little as needed. Any beginner might need a higher number of matches to gain more experience. It is recommended to keep the initial scouting Probe alife as long as possible, if need be, another one might be sacrificed to replace the initial Probe. 
</p><p><br />
</p>

<dl>
<dt>Execution
</dt>
</dl>
<p>Fast Expansion can be performed without an early Forge, if the map allows it. On outdated maps like <a href="/starcraft/Gods_Garden" title="Gods Garden" class="mw-redirect">Gods Garden</a> the backdoor expansion can be secured with Zealots blocking the ramp. Zealot/Gateway first openings rely on good micromanagement and are in general riskier and harder to perform. The strength of these kind of openings lie in their ability to launch earlier attacks on Zerg, thus making it more difficult for the opponent to counter by building up a strong economy himself. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition">Transition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p><p>However, Protoss can follow different strategies based on his overall game plan. A great variety of timed attacks can be prepared.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counters">Counters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Counters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In general several all-in rushes can destroy a Fast Expansion. Examples would be <a href="/starcraft/9_Pool_Speed_(vs._Protoss)" title="9 Pool Speed (vs. Protoss)">Speedling rushes</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Zerg_vs._Protoss_Guide#All-in_Hydralisks" title="Zerg vs. Protoss Guide">Hydralis busts</a> or <a href="/starcraft/Zerg_vs._Protoss_Guide#All-in_Mutalisks" title="Zerg vs. Protoss Guide">two Hatch Mutalisk</a> builds. These can be defended by adapting the Build Order and need good scouting information.
</p><p>The Fast Expansions can be soft countered by two Hatchery before Spawning Pool openings. These exploit the lack of Zealots in the first minutes of the game. Protoss can counter with an even faster Tech or a massive attack in between the early game and the mid game. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Maps">Notable Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notable Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Fast Expansion Build Orders can be used on almost any maps. However, some outdated maps have relatively wide choke points, making a good wall-in nearly impossible, thus offering more options to Zerg to successfully perform a strong early-on attack. Prime examples would be <a href="/starcraft/Luna" title="Luna">Luna</a>'s 11 o'clock Position, or the 12 o'clock spawning points on <a href="/starcraft/Lost_Temple" title="Lost Temple">Lost Temple</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Python" title="Python">Python</a>. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
