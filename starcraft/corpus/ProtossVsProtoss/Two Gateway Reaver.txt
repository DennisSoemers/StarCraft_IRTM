<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Gate_Reaver_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "<b>Two Gateway Reaver</b>" strategies refer to a category of early and mid game strategies. All openings enable a player to train an army of Dragoons with Range Upgrade and a Shuttle with a Reaver. The Reaver is used to support the ground forces, rather than to drop the opponent fast. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution">Execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Gate_Reaver_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first Zealots trained in the variations described in this article can be used to harass the opponent, or to defend the ramp. Against Two Gateway Zealot openings more Zealots might be added and the Reaver tech can be delayed. Against Dark Templars, the Observer can be prioritized. However, most Dark Templar openings have a designed attack timing of the first Dark Templar around six minutes. The Observer, in most variations, will be ready around six minutes and twenty seconds. If the Choke Point / entrance / ramp to the Protoss main is blocked by Dragoons or Zealots, the Dark Templar attack can be denied easily. 
</p><p>Usually the army of Dragoons and Reavers is used to expand after the first Reaver was trained. Depending on the Build Order of the opponent, the Nexus might be built sooner or later. Especially 4 Gateway Dragoon openings punish an early expansion. In this scenario, the entire Build Order needs to be adopted. Only on maps like <a href="/starcraft/Destination" title="Destination">Destination</a> an expansion might be successful. 
</p><p>Depending on the opponent's army the Dragoon / Reaver combination can be used to gain map control. The higher the opponent's Dragoon count is, the more carefully a player should act. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counters">Counters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Gate_Reaver_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Counters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Two Gateway Reaver opening is hard countered by <a href="/starcraft/4_Gate_Goon_(vs._Protoss)" title="4 Gate Goon (vs. Protoss)">4 Gate Dragoons</a> on most maps. Other than that, it's considered to be a safe way to soft counter Dark Templar openings. 
</p><p>This build can also be used to pressure players opening with a Fast Expansion, if the Reavers are microed carefully and the first five Zealot rush can be defended well. Furthermore, the Two Gateway Reaver opening is equally good compared with other openings like 3 Gateway Dragoon Observers.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Maps">Notable Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Gate_Reaver_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notable Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Two Gateway Reaver opening works generally on all maps. Maps with difficult Natural Expansion entrances like <a href="/starcraft/Destination" title="Destination">Destination</a> support the build, as Reavers can be used easier to defend a second Nexus against a greater number of Dragoons. 
</p><p><br />
