<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=4_Gate_Goon_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "<b>Four Gateway Dragoon</b>" opening is one of the most aggressive and commiting strategies in the Protoss mirror match up. It enables a player to produce a great amount of Dragoons early on, while putting him in a bigger economical disadvantage. 
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution">Execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=4_Gate_Goon_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Four Gateway Dragoon opening works best against an unprepared opponent. The timing is designed that the first attack arrives at the enemy's base around 6 minutes or thirty seconds later. However, the exact timing does depend on the map. The great amount of Dragoons is usually enough to literally walk over the opponent, if he did not anticipate the large scaled attack. 
</p><p>It is important that the Probe production is stopped early in the game. At the 27 Supply mark, no more Probes will be produced. The Probe production can be resumed once the first attack started. However, if the initial attack does not kill the opponent or inflicts a great amount of damage, the economical disadvantage is usually to big to recover from.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counters">Counters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=4_Gate_Goon_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Counters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Dark Templar opening punishes the Four Gateway Dragoon opening directly. The lack of Observers make it impossible to react in time. Any three Gateway Dragoon opening or a two Gateway Dragoon Opening and Reavers might hard counter this strategy as well, if the choke point does not allow all attacking Dragoons to shoot. This is the case for maps which feature ramps to the opponent's main base and a backdoor expansion, such as <a href="/starcraft/Outsider" title="Outsider">Outsider</a>.
</p><p>The opening can hard counter any opening which aims to fast expand or which does tech on units other than Dark Templars. The attacking army after six minutes can out-micro any other combination easily. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Maps">Notable Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=4_Gate_Goon_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notable Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The opening heavily depends on the maps. Maps with large chokes and reverse ramps to the opponent's Natural Expansion support the opening. Examples would be <a href="/starcraft/Blue_Storm" title="Blue Storm">Blue Storm</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Heartbreak_Ridge" title="Heartbreak Ridge">Heartbreak Ridge</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Medusa" title="Medusa">Medusa</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Colosseum_II" title="Colosseum II" class="mw-redirect">Colosseum II</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Bloody_Ridge" title="Bloody Ridge">Bloody Ridge</a> or <a href="/starcraft/Python" title="Python">Python</a>. 
</p><p>Maps with difficult pathing, backdoor expansions and narrow Choke Points generally work against this strategy. Examples would be <a href="/starcraft/Outsider" title="Outsider">Outsider</a> or <a href="/starcraft/Destination" title="Destination">Destination</a>. 
</p>
