	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading">Walling as Terran</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent">

<p>Walling (or "walling in", "wall-in" or "blocking") means creating buildings at the ramp or opening of your base so that enemy units can't get through. Walls are commonly seen in TvP, TvZ, and 2v2 games. They are generally built using the first <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a>, the <a href="/starcraft/Barracks" title="Barracks">Barracks</a>, and if necessary the second <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a>. In TvZ, it is also common to use a <a href="/starcraft/Factory" title="Factory">Factory</a> as a part of the wall. This quick guide will show you the basic principles of constructing a wall on any map.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Why_Wall.3F">Why Wall?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Why Wall?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walls are a powerful tool for Terran players as they allow Terran to guard against most early rushes done by a Protoss or Zerg player. A cautious Terran player that walls every game will be safe against most early rushes, provided that he repairs the wall. There are some early builds that are designed to take down a Terran wall (such as the <a href="/starcraft/10/15_Gate" title="10/15 Gate" class="mw-redirect">10/15 Gate</a> from Protoss), but they will generally work best on maps like <a href="/starcraft/Medusa" title="Medusa">Medusa</a>, where there is no ramp for Terran to hold.
</p><p>Yet one of the most important functions of walling is to deny scouting. Thus, you can constantly keep your opponent guessing in TvP and TvZ, which is vital to ensure that you can pull off any unorthodox or rush strategies in the early game.
</p><p>It is important to note that your economy will be a little bit worse off if you wall than if you don't as it takes time for SCVs to travel to your wall and back to the mineral line. However, the benefits of walling are generally worth the few minerals lost due to walling.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_placements">Building placements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Building placements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tight_against_Zealots">Tight against Zealots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tight against Zealots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The key is to have the <b>top</b> or the <b>right</b> side of the <a href="/starcraft/Barracks" title="Barracks">Barracks</a> edge against a <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a>. This way <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> can't get through between those buildings
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> can't get through 2 <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depots</a> that share edges or corners
</li>
<li>Where the buildings edge the maps, you generally want to try to make as much of the buildings as possible to be close to the edge. The wall generally gets tight if you fit the corner of the <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Barracks" title="Barracks">Barracks</a> into a place where the edge of the cliff turns a little. This comes with experience though.
</li>
<li>The left side of the <a href="/starcraft/Factory" title="Factory">Factory</a> against the <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a> will keep <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> out.
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tight_against_Zerglings">Tight against Zerglings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tight against Zerglings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Only the <b>top</b> side of the <a href="/starcraft/Barracks" title="Barracks">Barracks</a> against the <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a> will keep <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> out
</li>
<li>Both the <b>bottom</b> and the <b>left</b> side of the <a href="/starcraft/Factory" title="Factory">Factory</a> against the <a href="/starcraft/Barracks" title="Barracks">Barracks</a> will keep <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> out
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Factory" title="Factory">Factory</a> on top of the <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depot</a> and vice-versa will keep <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> out
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> can't get through <a href="/starcraft/Supply_Depot" title="Supply Depot">Supply Depots</a> stacked on top of each other
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> can often get through around the edge where <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> can't
</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tight_against_Scarabs">Tight against Scarabs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tight against Scarabs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Walling off scarabs improves the chance of path-finding glitches by increasing the distance of the shortest path. This provides more time to maneuver units than without the wall-off, often resulting in scarabs moving back and forth until timeout.
</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Machine_Shop" title="Machine Shop">Machine Shop</a> to the left of an <a href="/starcraft/Armory" title="Armory">Armory</a>.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Control_Tower" title="Control Tower">Control Tower</a> to the left of an <a href="/starcraft/Armory" title="Armory">Armory</a>.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Armory" title="Armory">Armory</a> adjacent to mineral patch.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Academy" title="Academy">Academy</a> to the left of a mineral patch.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Machine_Shop" title="Machine Shop">Machine Shop</a> to the left of a mineral patch.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Armory" title="Armory">Armory</a> below a <a href="/starcraft/Refinery" title="Refinery">Refinery</a>.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Comsat_Station" title="Comsat Station">Comsat Station</a> to the left of an <a href="/starcraft/Armory" title="Armory">Armory</a>.
</li>
<li><a href="/starcraft/Missile_Turret" title="Missile Turret">Missile Turret</a> below mineral patch.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Testing_your_wall">Testing your wall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Testing your wall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is a simple rule of thumb for testing your wall in-game
</p>
<ul>
<li>If an <a href="/starcraft/SCV" title="SCV">SCV</a> can get through, so can a <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealot</a>.
</li>
<li>If a <a href="/starcraft/Marine" title="Marine">Marine</a> can get through, so can a <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zergling</a>. However, note that the opposite is sometimes false; a Zergling can sometimes fit where a Marine cannot. Therefore, manually testing your walls with Zerglings is encouraged, or strictly following the guidelines laid out in the "Tight against Zerglings" section.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fixing_a_failed_wall">Fixing a failed wall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Walling_as_Terran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fixing a failed wall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>If it's critical that your wall doesn't allow enemy units to pass, as in a 2v2 where you scout twenty <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a>/<a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> coming toward you and you just discovered your wall is not tight, the easiest way is to fix it with one or more <a href="/starcraft/SCV" title="SCV">SCVs</a>. 
</p>
<ul>
<li>Place the <a href="/starcraft/SCV" title="SCV">SCV</a> in the exact position where units get through.
</li>
<li>Select the <a href="/starcraft/SCV" title="SCV">SCV</a> together with a <a href="/starcraft/Marine" title="Marine">Marine</a> and click Hold Position.
</li>
<li>Optionally, use a second <a href="/starcraft/SCV" title="SCV">SCV</a> to repair the first.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Now your opponent has to manually target the <a href="/starcraft/SCV" title="SCV">SCV</a>, and if there are some <a href="/starcraft/Marine" title="Marine">Marines</a> behind the wall, it is rarely worth it.
</p>
