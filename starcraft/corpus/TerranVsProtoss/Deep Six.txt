<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Deep_Six_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The  "<b>Deep Six</b>" is an exotic and highly risky Terran mid game strategy. It is designed to punish greedy Protoss expansion behavior in a critical point with a switch from Factory based units to Barrack "bio" forces. However, it is categorized as all-in, as the timed attack does not allow either side to fail and can only rarely be transitioned out of in most games. Furthermore, concealing the strategy is nearly impossible and a lot of factors must coincide before the strategy can be put to work efficiently. 
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution">Execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Deep_Six_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scouting information is pivotal for this game. Whenever Protoss expands conservative or opens with an early Templar Archive, transitioning into Deep Six is not recommended. Furthermore, the exact scouting of the opponent does matter as well. Any Observer needs to be killed before adding the Barracks. Consequently, the most important timing is to build the Turret ring around the base. It should be impossible for Protoss to anticipate the transition into Medics and Marines. This includes hiding the Academy and the Engineering Bay out of vision range of the Protoss army. Their lights flashing indicate upgrades for bio units and therefore enable the Protoss to counter the timing attack easily. 
</p><p>The army composition is slightly different from the ordinary Medic/Marine composition against Zerg. More Medics can be added to the army, as Marines are under stronger fire later on during the attack. The attack itself is straight forward and should be performed without any stops along the way. Tanks should only be sieged when the ultimate fight is about to happen, otherwise the attack force should be constantly moving. Marines should not be damaged by Stim Packs too much as well, as it drastically decreases their potential in fights against Dragoons. High Templars and Reavers are the high priority targets in fights. 
</p><p><br /> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counters">Counters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Deep_Six_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Counters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Almost all Protoss strategies may counter this build order. A larger Dragoon force, early speed zealots, Dark Templars, Reavers and especially High Templars kill the incoming bio force if microed properly. The Build Order only hard counters rather greedy play, such as sloppily performed Two Base Arbiter, Two Base Carrier or really fast two Expansion openings. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Maps">Notable Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Deep_Six_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notable Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maps with large choke points support the large bio attack, while narrow passages work against a fast moving push. Choke Points can be abused by Protoss to stall incoming attacks, force Marines to funnel and so on. Also, whenever Protoss can abuse various high ground passages, such as on Sniper Ridge, the Build Order loses efficiency.
</p>
