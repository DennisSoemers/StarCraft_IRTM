<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=1_Factory_Double_Expand_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "<b>One Factory Double Expand</b>" is a an early to mid game transition Build Order performed by Terran against a Fast Expanding (Nexus first) Protoss. It aims to raise a strong economy early on, abusing several disadvantages Nexus first openings have. The Korean professional <a href="/starcraft/Boxer" title="Boxer" class="mw-redirect">Boxer</a> popularized the build in 2006. 
</p><p><br />
</p>

<dl>
<dt>Potential Adaptations
</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>Gas Cut at 16/18 Supply is fixed. However, the SCVs are only sent back to mine once the Factory finished if there is <b>no</b> gas at the second Expansion spot. If the second expansion has a Geyser, the SCVs can be kept mining minerals after the second Command Center.
</li>
<li>Siege Mode can be delayed if Protoss goes for late Observers. Instead an additional Siege Tank is built and another Factory added.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution">Execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=1_Factory_Double_Expand_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Build Order is reactionary and depends on scouting information; if a Protoss does not open with a Nexus first Build Order, the strategy itself will be rendered useless. 
</p><p>In the early game the Terran follows the ordinary strategy of a Siege Expansion. However, instead of adding Siege Tanks, he will train Vultures early on. Vultures and a few Marines are then used to drive back any potential Protoss harassment and scouting Probes. Once the Spider Mines were upgraded, the Vultures are used to place Mines in important locations - the entrance to the Protoss Natural Expansion and the Choke Points near the own (Terran's) expansions. Since Protoss went for a Nexus first, he will be forced to give up map control, because the Observatory will be delayed significantly. However, Mines might be defused manually with Dragoon Hold-Position Micromanagement. In this case, it is recommended to place mine on top of high grounds and bridges to increase the potential of Protoss failing to manually defuse Mines.
</p><p>As soon as the second Command Center is being built, acquiring more Siege Tanks and Siege Mode is essential. Sooner or later Protoss will be able to attack. With this in mind, the location of the second Command Center / the second expansion matters. It should not be in direct path from Protoss to Terran base, as this makes it easier for Protoss to perform a timed attack. Furthermore, Turrets should be built to defend against incoming drops and Dragoon/Shuttle attacks. 
</p><p>Once the two bases are fortified, Terran can transition into ordinary mid game strategies, such as a 2/1 or +1 Weapon Timing Attack. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Maps">Notable Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=1_Factory_Double_Expand_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The location of the third expansion in relation to the spawning positions determine the usability of the strategy. Maps with wide open third bases like Python are usually the worst choice to use this reactionary Build. Instead maps like <a href="/starcraft/Circuit_Breaker" title="Circuit Breaker">Circuit Breaker</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Electric_Circuit" title="Electric Circuit">Electric Circuit</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Sniper_Ridge" title="Sniper Ridge">Sniper Ridge</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Fighting_Spirit" title="Fighting Spirit">Fighting Spirit</a> support the strategy. Each of them features easy access for Terran and difficult terrain for Protoss to manually defuse Spider Mines. 
</p><p><br />
</p>
