<p>The 2 Rax build is a build that was very popular before the introduction of the <a href="/starcraft/1_Rax_FE_(vs._Zerg)" title="1 Rax FE (vs. Zerg)">1 Rax FE</a>. Modern adaptations of the build generally use this build to force the Zerg to spend a significant amount of money on Sunken Colonies by faking a Marine/Medic push and then, instead of continuing to pump units as in older versions of this build, taking an expansion to enter a macro game. In addition, it allows for an easier defense against the <a href="/starcraft/9_Pool_(vs_Zerg)" title="9 Pool (vs Zerg)" class="mw-redirect">9 Pool</a> opening.
</p><p>This build is the original incarnation of TvZ fast expand made by iloveoov in 2003. To this day it remains very strong against 2 Hatchery builds and is underused in this regard. However, because of the slower Command Center and oftentimes useless early second Barrack, it is significantly weaker than 1 rax FE against 3 Hatchery Mutalisks, which caused its overall decline in popularity.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strong">Strong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Rax_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Strong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is a strong build on maps where Zergs normally 2 Hatch. Some examples include <a href="/starcraft/Destination" title="Destination">Destination</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Raid_Assault" title="Raid Assault">Raid Assault</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weak">Weak</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Rax_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Weak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is a weaker build on any map where Zergs normally 3 Hatch. Some examples include <a href="/starcraft/Arcadia" title="Arcadia">Arcadia</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Loki" title="Loki">Loki</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mentality">Mentality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Rax_FE_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mentality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The goal of this build is to force the Zerg to overinvest in Sunken Colonies, fearing a sunken break, while the Terran expands instead. Remember that this build does not look any different from a <a href="/starcraft/Sparks_Terran" title="Sparks Terran">Sparks Rush</a>. Therefore, if the Terran allows the Zerg to see neither their base nor their full army size, the Zerg is forced to assume that they are doing a Sparks Rush and overcompensate on defense in response. Having to build 4-5 Sunken Colonies in a <a href="/starcraft/2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Terran)" title="2 Hatch Muta (vs. Terran)">2 Hatch Muta</a> build is very taxing on the economy and if they skimp on defense they can easily be punished.
</p>
