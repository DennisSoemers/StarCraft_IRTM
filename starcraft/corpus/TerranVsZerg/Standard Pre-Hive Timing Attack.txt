<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mentality">Mentality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mentality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As mentioned above, the purpose of the build is to pressure the Zerg's natural just before his defiler can defend with dark swarm. While this attack can very well end the game on its own, it most definitely does not need to. Against an equally skilled player it will force the Zerg to quit making drones. Do some damage and then transition into SK Terran.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order_Clarification">Build Order Clarification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Build Order Clarification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".28Tech_SCV.29">(Tech SCV)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: (Tech SCV)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The SCV used to make this Rax is that same one used for most of your tech buildings, one right after the other. He makes the E-bay, Factory, Starport and Science Facility.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".28Turrets.29">(Turrets)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: (Turrets)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is the optimal turret timing vs 3 Hatch Muta.  If the Zerg player is going 2 Hatch Muta, build them soon after your E-bay.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Moving_Out">Moving Out</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Moving Out">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At this point you should have 1 vessel, 3 tanks, around 2 groups of marines and around five medics depending on the effectiveness of the Zerg player's muta harass. Scan the Zerg's base to ensure they are teching to Hive. If they are, move out and pressure his 3rd or natural. If not, scout for extra expansions and turtle until they expand or tech to hive.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order_Changes_and_Adaptations">Build Order Changes and Adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Build Order Changes and Adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="4.2C5.2C6_Pool">4,5,6 Pool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 4,5,6 Pool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>These builds tend to get an extremely early Pool with the intention of killing you at the start. See <a href="/starcraft/Terran_Counter_to_4_Pool" title="Terran Counter to 4 Pool">Terran Counter to 4 Pool</a> in order to get an idea of how to counter such play. If you are able to block these builds, you almost certainly will win as long as you are cautious, and there is no longer a need to follow the 9min Push Build Order, or even take an Expansion. The most common follow ups to a 4,5, or 6 Pool build is 1 Hatch Tech (most commonly lurker), so be sure to get an early Academy/Engineering Bay.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="9_Pool">9 Pool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 9 Pool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>If the Zerg opts for a relatively early pool, often, the Zerglings will arrive at your base when you have 2 or less Marines. See <a href="/starcraft/Terran_Counter_to_9_Pool" title="Terran Counter to 9 Pool">Terran Counter to 9 Pool</a> for information on blocking it. However, if successfully blocked, you are significantly ahead of Zerg heading into the mid-game. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="3_Hatch_Speedling">3 Hatch Speedling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Standard_Pre-Hive_Timing_Attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 3 Hatch Speedling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>If an experienced Zerg decides to go 3 Hatch Zergling against you, you will often not know until it is too late. Therefore, it's important to keep an SCV outside of his base and be on constant alert for Zerglings streaming into your expansion. If you see an abnormally large amount of Zerglings kill your attempts at scouting, be even more on the alert, and consider building a bunker. In the event that you can't scout the build, as is oftentimes the case, pull your Marines back up the ramp or back into a smaller choke, pull all of your SCVs back into your main, and lift your Command Center immediately. If you don't do these things quickly, there is a high probability that you will lose the game. When the pulled SCVs reach your ramp or choke point, stop them and use them to block the Zerglings from attacking your Marines. An advanced strategy is to repeatedly move your SCVs to a mineral patch and then immediately stop them. This often messes up the Zergling AI and will cause them to move around aimlessly. 
</p><p>After this encounter Zerg will have essentially have lost the game, as they do not have the tech to fight Marines with Stim and Medics. Once you have a good amount of Marines, move out towards his Natural Expansion. If he has put enough Sunken Colonies up to block your attack, then you will be significantly ahead in the mid game and if he decides to be risky and put a minimal amount of defense in favor of tech, he will lose to your attack.
</p>
