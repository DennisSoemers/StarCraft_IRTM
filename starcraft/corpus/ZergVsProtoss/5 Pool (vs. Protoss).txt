<p>"5 Pool" is the most aggressive build a <a href="/starcraft/Zerg" title="Zerg">Zerg</a> player can do to <a href="/starcraft/Protoss" title="Protoss">Protoss</a>. The goal of this build is to catch a <a href="/starcraft/Protoss" title="Protoss">Protoss</a> player off guard with extremely early <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a>, and either win the game outright or prevent a <a href="/starcraft/Protoss_FE_(vs._Zerg)" title="Protoss FE (vs. Zerg)">Fast Expand</a>. "4 pool"s are never used versus Protoss, while both 4 and 5 pools are used vs. <a href="/starcraft/Terran" title="Terran">Terran</a> or <a href="/starcraft/Zerg" title="Zerg">Zerg</a>. Due to the damage caused to the Zerg economy by a 5 pool opening, it is often considered an all-in move, although it does have limited capability for transitions into the mid-game.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order">Build Order</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=5_Pool_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Build Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>4 Morph 1 <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drone</a>, you should be left with 5/9 control.
</li>
<li>5 Morph a <a href="/starcraft/Spawning_Pool" title="Spawning Pool">Pool</a>, you should be left with 4/9 control.
</li>
<li>4 Morph another <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drone</a>, you should be left with 5/9 control.
</li>
<li>5 Morph another <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drone</a>, you should be left with 6/9 control.
</li>
<li>6 Morph 3 sets of <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> with the <a href="/starcraft/Larva_(Unit)" title="Larva (Unit)">larvae</a>,  you should be left with 9/9 control.
</li>
</ul>
<p><b>Additional:</b>
</p><p><br />
</p><p>When the <a href="/starcraft/Zerglings" title="Zerglings" class="mw-redirect">Zerglings</a> are about 80% morphed, another larva will spawn and you have a few options:
</p>
<ul>
<li>9 Morph <a href="/starcraft/Extractor" title="Extractor">Extractor</a> (8/9 control) and morph another set of zerglings. Then cancel the Extractor, you should be left with 10/9 control.
</li>
</ul>
<p>OR
</p>
<ul>
<li>9 Morph <a href="/starcraft/Extractor" title="Extractor">Extractor</a> (8/9 control) and morph another Drone. Then cancel the Extractor, you should be left with 10/9 control.
</li>
</ul>
<p>OR
</p>
<ul>
<li>9 Morph <a href="/starcraft/Overlord" title="Overlord">Overlord</a>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order_Clarification">Build Order Clarification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=5_Pool_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Build Order Clarification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extractor_Trick">Extractor Trick</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=5_Pool_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Extractor Trick">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After your first 6 <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> hatch you have a choice. If you feel confident in your rush, you can use the <a href="/starcraft/Gas_Trick" title="Gas Trick" class="mw-redirect">Gas Trick</a> to quickly reinforce your initial <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> with an extra two. 
</p><p>If you feel like continuing your economy right away, either save up and create an <a href="/starcraft/Overlord" title="Overlord">Overlord</a> immediately, or you can use the <a href="/starcraft/Gas_Trick" title="Gas Trick" class="mw-redirect">Gas Trick</a> to get an extra <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drone</a> before the <a href="/starcraft/Overlord" title="Overlord">Overlord</a>. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scouting">Scouting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=5_Pool_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Scouting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Depending on how many positions are on the map, you shouldn't need to waste a scout to find the <a href="/starcraft/Protoss" title="Protoss">Protoss</a>. When playing on large four player maps like <a href="/starcraft/Python" title="Python">Python</a>, if you don't find the player with your <a href="/starcraft/Overlord" title="Overlord">Overlord</a> in the beginning, it's worth it to send out a <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drone</a> scout to find out exactly where your opponent is. In this situation your economy is already almost non-existent, so your initial <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> are going to need to do as much damage as possible, and going to the wrong position in the beginning can completely ruin this build's chances for success.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition">Transition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=5_Pool_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Generally, by the time your <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a> get to your opponent's base, your 5 <a href="/starcraft/Spawning_Pool" title="Spawning Pool">Pool</a> will have been scouted already. You need to focus on taking down his economy (aka probes).
</p><p>If you manage to do some damage, but not kill your opponent, with your initial lings you have two ways to go:
</p>
<ul>
<li>Go fully all-in and keep on producing lings, going for the victory
</li>
<li>Make drones and try to keep your opponent from setting up his expansion.
</li>
</ul>
<p>If you do very little damage, you are very far behind and can expect to see some zealots in your base rather quickly. You could drag the game out but GG is recommended.
</p>
