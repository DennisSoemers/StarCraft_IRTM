<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A faster and riskier variation of the Three Hatch Spire build order in ZvP, focused on faster Mutalisks with the intent to inflict significant economic damage and harassment. The build order revolves around getting Gas before the 3rd Hatchery, and getting 6 Mutalisks out as quickly possible.
</p><p>This build requires excellent <a href="/starcraft/Mutalisk_Harassment" title="Mutalisk Harassment">Mutalisk Harassment</a> to effectively pick off Corsairs, lone Photon Cannons, High Templar, Dragoons, and Probes.  It is strongest on maps with close air distances and against Protoss FE, particularly if the Protoss scouted the 12 Hatch late and delayed their economy (and therefore Corsairs or Archons) by completing their Forge and possibly Photon Cannon(s)before their Nexus.
</p><p>Against a Protoss player who does not build Photon Cannons in their main base in time (due to denied scouting and skimping on defense), it can deal game-ending damage to their economy and <a href="/starcraft/Technology_tree" title="Technology tree">tech tree</a>.  Even if this does not occur, significant economic damage can still be dealt.  2 Hatch Muta is by no means all-in, and failure to deal great economic damage is acceptable if it at least forces the Protoss to play defensively until the Zerg can ramp up their economy.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order_Clarification">Build Order Clarification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Build Order Clarification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Save_Larvae">Save Larvae</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Save Larvae">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stop making units when the Spawning Pool is at 300 HP.  It will then finish with 3 Larvae to be made into Zerglings.
</p><p>Be sure to make at least two Overlords directly before your spire hits 350 HP, so that you have 6 Larva to be made into Mutalisks, and extra cap to make more.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Make_Drones_When_Possible">Make Drones When Possible</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Make Drones When Possible">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the initial 8 Zerglings, you cannot afford to build any more because of the hungry nature of this build. Use the Zerglings to threaten a runby, deny scouting, and pick off stray Zealots to avoid having to deal with the Protoss army directly.  This will allow you to make no further Zerglings or Sunken Colonies, and focus purely on Drone production.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Send_Drone_to_Take_3rd_Base">Send Drone to Take 3rd Base</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Send Drone to Take 3rd Base">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The exact timing to send out a drone will vary depending on the map and the distance to the base you wish to take.  The goal is for the Drone to arrive at exactly 300 minerals after 26/26 supply.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition">Transition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frequently 2 Hatch Muta must transition into more standard play.  After 11 Mutalisks, it is advisable to put down a Hydralisk Den and transition to Lurker/Ling/Scourge or Hydra/Ling/Muta.  While harassing and researching for the transition, Drones must be powered to make up for the early economic disadvantage.  While powering Drones, extra Gas should be diverted to upgrades, beginning with Air Carapace, and moving to ground upgrades once Evolution Chambers are morphed.
</p><p>Depending on the damage dealt to the Protoss as well as their build, Overlord Speed and/or Burrow may need to be researched, and Sunken Colonies may be necessary to defend against a counterattack or Dark Templar harassment.
</p><p>It should be noted that should the Protoss player have adequate defenses in place, the transition phase of this build is highly mechanically demanding.  Harassing against Cannon/Corsair or Cannon/Archon requires significant attention to avoid losses, but great effort must also be devoted to powering Drones: due to cutting Drones early on, diminishing returns on mining has not been reached and therefore avoiding idle Larvae and/or Drones is critical.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Countered_By">Countered By</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Countered By">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corsair.2FReaver"><a href="/starcraft/Sair/Reaver_(vs._Zerg)" title="Sair/Reaver (vs. Zerg)" class="mw-redirect">Corsair/Reaver</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Corsair/Reaver">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Playing against Corsair/Reaver is an uphill battle as Corsairs are an effective counter to Mutalisks in sufficient numbers.  This is made worse if the Protoss opens with double Stargate, as critical mass will be achieved twice as quickly.  Also, because Corsair/Reaver is not mineral intensive, the Protoss player will have plenty of money to spare on adequate cannon defenses.  It is critical to use Mutalisk/Scourge micro to either keep the Corsair count low or force them back to enable harass as long as possible while you make the transition to Hydralisks.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Counter_To">Counter To</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Counter To">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".2B1_Speedlot_Rush"><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Plus1_Speedzeal_(vs._Zerg)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plus1 Speedzeal (vs. Zerg) (page does not exist)">+1 Speedlot Rush</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: +1 Speedlot Rush">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Zealots cannot harm Mutalisks, so defending against a +1 Speedlot rush is primarily a matter of mitigating the economic damage they deal while Mutalisks clean them up.  Secondarily, the Protoss player is also trying to buy time while they set up Photon Cannons and tech to Corsairs and/or Archons.  Typically, you will want to put down a sunken at your natural and continue Mutalisk production until 11 while massing Zerglings to draw Zealot fire.  At the same time, you must micro your Drones to minimize losses and your Mutalisks to kill the Zealots efficiently.  If you minimize damage to your economy, you will be ahead as the Protoss will have spent significant minerals on the lost Zealots and the necessary Photon Cannons.
</p>
