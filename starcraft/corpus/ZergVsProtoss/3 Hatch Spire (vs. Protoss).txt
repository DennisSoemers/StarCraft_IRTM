<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This build opens very similarly to a <a href="/starcraft/3_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Terran)" title="3 Hatch Muta (vs. Terran)">ZvT 3 Hatch Mutalisk build</a>. It looks to get <a href="/starcraft/Scourge" title="Scourge">Scourge</a> and/or <a href="/starcraft/Mutalisk" title="Mutalisk">Mutalisks</a> out very quickly to control Protoss' early midgame Tech, allowing Zerg the freedom to power <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drones</a> through the <a href="/starcraft/Mid_game" title="Mid game">midgame</a>. It can sometimes be used as an aggressive strategy by catching the Protoss main off guard while it has very few <a href="/starcraft/Photon_Cannon" title="Photon Cannon">Cannons</a> and minimal air defense.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Build_Order">Build Order</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Build Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clarification">Clarification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Clarification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="12_-_Zerglings_.286.29">12 - Zerglings (6)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 12 - Zerglings (6)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>If you have scouted your opponent by this time, it may be possible to skip these Zerglings and spawn Drones instead. If you go this route, you should still make 2 Zerglings to kill the scouting <a href="/starcraft/Probe" title="Probe">Probe</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".40100_Gas_-_Natural_Extractor_.28optional.29">@100 Gas - Natural Extractor (optional)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: @100 Gas - Natural Extractor (optional)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By getting a natural geyser, you focus some midgame economy for more early game options; you allow yourself more Scourge against <a href="/starcraft/Stargate" title="Stargate">Stargate</a> openings and Mutalisks against +1 openings. If you are planning on transitioning straight into <a href="/starcraft/Lurker" title="Lurker">Lurker</a>/Ling, you should get the natural Extractor. You should also get it on a map where Stargate openings are popular and powerful (namely <a href="/starcraft/Andromeda" title="Andromeda">Andromeda</a>). If you will transition into <a href="/starcraft/Hydralisk" title="Hydralisk">Hydralisks</a> before Lurkers, you may forgo the natural Extractor.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".40150_Gas_-_Overlord_Speed_.28optional.29">@150 Gas - Overlord Speed (optional)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: @150 Gas - Overlord Speed (optional)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Against Stargate openings, Overlord Speed is usually recommended. If you feel confident in your Overlord placement, you can skip it, although doing so does limit your midgame options and may offer great benefit. Against +1 builds you can delay it as you see fit.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="33_-_2_to_4_Overlords">33 - 2 to 4 Overlords</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 33 - 2 to 4 Overlords">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>If your opponent did a Stargate opening, you will lose at least one Overlord to the first <a href="/starcraft/Corsair" title="Corsair">Corsair</a>; you want enough Overlords to cover those losses when your Spire finishes. Not being able to make Scourge right as the Spire finishes is disastrous. If your opponent opened +1 <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealot</a> speed, you need enough supply to make 9 Mutalisks as the Spire finishes (Either 33/51 or 35/59).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".40100.25_Spire_-_Scourge_OR_Mutalisks">@100% Spire - Scourge OR Mutalisks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: @100% Spire - Scourge OR Mutalisks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Against Corsair openings, make enough Scourge (typically 4-8 depending on the map and <a href="/starcraft/Gateway" title="Gateway">Gateway</a> count) to completely negate your opponents Corsairs. Send an initial pair of Scourge into your opponent's base to check his/her Tech (<a href="/starcraft/Robotics_Facility" title="Robotics Facility">Robotics</a>? Many Gateways? Continual Corsair production? Third base expansion attempt?) so you may counter accordingly.
</p><p>Against +1 openings, make 9-11 Mutalisks to deal the damage, and enough <a href="/starcraft/Sunken_Colony" title="Sunken Colony">Sunken Colonies</a> and Zerglings to absorb the attack while stopping a potential run by.
</p><p>Either way, this is <i>not</i> a Scourge / Mutalisk timing attack looking to finish your opponent. That build order is slightly modified and is a very high level build which should not really be attempted by lower level players. You should still use your Mutalisks to harass your opponent's main base and <a href="/starcraft/Mineral_Line" title="Mineral Line">Mineral Lines</a> whenever possible, but use the Mutalisks more so to gain map control while you power economy through the midgame.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition">Transition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=3_Hatch_Spire_(vs._Protoss)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After your Scourge or Mutalisks are out, immediately add a <a href="/starcraft/Hydralisk_Den" title="Hydralisk Den">Hydralisk Den</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Evolution_Chamber" title="Evolution Chamber">Evolution Chamber</a> (or two), research Zergling Speed, and place another Hatchery (at your third base or a fourth base). Once Overlord Speed completes, take another look at your opponent's base while spreading Overlords across the map at all choke points and potential expansions. Follow up with either Lurkers or Hydralisk Speed / Range. With no Corsairs to kill Overlords and with Overlord Speed complete, Protoss should not be able to take a third base quickly without being run over by Lurker/Ling or Hydra/Ling. Take this time to continue powering Drones while you move towards <a href="/starcraft/Hive" title="Hive">Hive</a>.
</p>
