<p>This build sacrifices economy in exchange for faster <a href="/starcraft/Lurker" title="Lurker">Lurkers</a>. This build is more all-in than the 2 Hatch Muta build because it is difficult to damage Terran unless Terran's natural completely goes down. Therefore, this build is less commonly seen than <a href="/starcraft/2_Hatch_Muta_(vs._Terran)" title="2 Hatch Muta (vs. Terran)">2 Hatch Mutas</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="12_Hatch_Variation">12 Hatch Variation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 12 Hatch Variation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 12 Hatch Variation aims to get a stronger economy. This allows for more variation in the build, including the possibility to transition into normal play and also to possibly break the Terran's natural using Speedlings.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="12_Hatch_Variation_Build_Order">12 Hatch Variation Build Order</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 12 Hatch Variation Build Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</li>
<li> 10 - <a href="/starcraft/Extractor" title="Extractor">Extractor</a> (13, if playing economically)
</li>
<li> NOTE: 3 <a href="/starcraft/Drone" title="Drone">Drones</a> after pool for 6(+2) <a href="/starcraft/Zergling" title="Zergling">Zerglings</a>, 4 drones after pool for 4(+2) Lings
</li>
<li> 16 - Overlord
</li>
<li> @100 Gas - <a href="/starcraft/Lair" title="Lair">Lair</a>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="12_Pool_Variation">12 Pool Variation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 12 Pool Variation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Drop_Variation">Drop Variation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Drop Variation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the all-in nature of the build, it is not uncommon to see Zergs dropping 2 Lurkers into the Terran's base with a slow Overlord. In this variation, the 12 Hatch variation is used, and the only difference is that after the Lair finishes, drop tech is started. Make sure to place an Overlord in position with 2 Lurkers once drop tech finishes. This build is most effective on maps where Overlords have only a short distance to move to reach the Terran main. This means that maps like <a href="/starcraft/Moon_Glaive" title="Moon Glaive">Moon Glaive</a> are good for this build.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Maps">Notable Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Notable Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strong">Strong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Strong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/starcraft/Medusa" title="Medusa">Medusa</a>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weak">Weak</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Weak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mentality">Mentality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Mentality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With your early Lurker/Ling force your goal should be to be aggressive and establish a contain while possibly dealing some damage depending on your micromangament ability and how much minerals the Terran invested in defense (no bunker or turrets as a sure sign to put additional pressure on the Terran). During this your <a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Third_Gas_Expansion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Third Gas Expansion (page does not exist)">Third Gas Expansion</a>.
</p><p>This build is also just one variation of the 2 Hatch Lurker build, and it should be kept in mind that your Terran opponent should be looking for an array of followups such as drop, Mutalisks or fast Hive. In other words, not letting him scout what you're doing is very beneficial.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition">Transition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=2_Hatch_Lurker_(vs._Terran)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Transition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>If you managed to set up a contain then the game should go into a 'standard' bionic Terran vs Zerg game. Following by saturating your third base and engaging the inevitable Terran Ball while you make your way to Defilers and Hive play.
</p>
