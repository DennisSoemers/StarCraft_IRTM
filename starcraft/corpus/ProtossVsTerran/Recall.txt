<p><i>"Recently the Protoss have found that the ability to rapidly bring more fighting forces to war zones is invaluable. The Recall ability creates a tear in the fabric of space-time, and any friendly units that enter the swirling vortex will instantaneously appear next to the Arbiter that initiated the effect."</i>
</p><p><b>Recall</b> is a spell cast by the <a href="/starcraft/Protoss" title="Protoss">Protoss</a> <a href="/starcraft/Arbiter" title="Arbiter">Arbiter</a>. It opens a 5x5 wormhole which teleports all friendly units in the wormhole to the caster. You cannot Recall allied units, only your own. You cannot Recall ground units onto spaces that is impassable to them normally. So you cannot Recall ground units into Water, Lava and so on. <sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use">Use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Recall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Recall is staple in PvT late game, as the instantenous transportation exploits the Terran's army's lack of mobility. In the PvP and PvZ match-up, Recall is less common. In both match-ups the high gas requirement of the Arbiter usually loses to the much needed <a href="/starcraft/High_Templar" title="High Templar">High Templars</a>. Also, these enemy's armies are mobile enough to catch up with your Recall, in which case your forces severed from your main army and are quickly taken out. In the rare case Recall is seen in these match-ups it is usually used for a tactical strike, such as taking out an island expansion.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PvT">PvT</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Recall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: PvT">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Whereas <a href="/starcraft/Stasis_Field" title="Stasis Field">Stasis Field</a> usually is the prioritised spell when dealing with a Terran army, <strong class="selflink">Recall</strong> may be researched if the Protoss is confident he can hold off the Terran push. A Recall can be devastating to a Terran main base, as key buildings can be taken out quickly. Recall is especially effective when dealing with island expansions as the Terran usually doesn't have the means to send a comparable force of reinforcements. 
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Considerations_when_recalling">Considerations when recalling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Recall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Considerations when recalling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Recall has a matrix of 138X133 Width X Height.
</p><p><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=File:60332914.jpg&amp;filetimestamp=20091117015557&amp;" class="image"><img alt="" src="/starcraft/images/a/a6/60332914.jpg" width="214" height="203" /></a>
</p><p>When recall is used in the center of this formation
</p><p><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=File:Ef71fe3a89.jpg&amp;filetimestamp=20091117015854&amp;" class="image"><img alt="" src="/starcraft/images/9/97/Ef71fe3a89.jpg" width="536" height="201" /></a>
</p><p>It is seen that the outer line of zealots was not recalled. This is because they are only partially within the 138X138 boundary of recall.
Units not entirely under this area are not affected.
</p><p><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=File:071d65b5f8.jpg&amp;filetimestamp=20091117020131&amp;" class="image"><img alt="" src="/starcraft/images/6/6d/071d65b5f8.jpg" width="610" height="321" /></a>
</p><p>In this one we can see that arbiter was able to recall a 138 Width of Zealots but only 128 Widht of Dragoons. This simply means that a unit has to be totally under the recall zone to be recalled.
</p><p>Final note:
Does not count the positions of unit before recall because after recall all positions will be mixed.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Counter">Counter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Recall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Counter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li> Often the Terran makes sure a <a href="/starcraft/Science_Vessel" title="Science Vessel">Science Vessel</a> is patrolling his vulnerable areas. An <a href="/starcraft/EMP_Shockwave" title="EMP Shockwave">EMP</a> can deplete the Arbiter's energy, making a Recall impossible. Alternatively, <a href="/starcraft/Lockdown" title="Lockdown">Lockdown</a> can be used to stop the incoming Arbiter in his tracks.
</li>
<li> When enough <a href="/starcraft/Missile_Turret" title="Missile Turret">Turrets</a> are in place, they can shoot down the Arbiter before it reaches the designated area. Even when the Arbiter gets off a Recall, it will be shot down, dispelling the cloaking field and making it easier to deal with the Protoss units.
</li>
<li> When expecting a Recall, Terrans do well to plant <a href="/starcraft/Spider_Mine" title="Spider Mine">Spider Mines</a> in their main. A Recall into a minefield will instantly turn the tide of the game as all mines go off instantaneously, massacring the Protoss forces.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Combination_with_Other_Spells">In Combination with Other Spells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Recall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In Combination with Other Spells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li> Any status changes such as <a href="/starcraft/Irradiate" title="Irradiate">Irradiate</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Lockdown" title="Lockdown">Lockdown</a>, <a href="/starcraft/Parasite" title="Parasite">Parasite</a> etc. remain active on Recalled units.
</li>
<li> Units that are <a href="/starcraft/Stasis" title="Stasis" class="mw-redirect">Stasised</a> or <a href="/starcraft/Burrow" title="Burrow">Burrowed</a> cannot be Recalled.
</li>
<li> <a href="/starcraft/Hallucination" title="Hallucination">Hallucinations</a> cannot be Recalled.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Recall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li> Arbiters can't Recall themselves.  
</li>
<li> <a href="/starcraft/Larva" title="Larva">Larvae</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Egg" title="Egg">Eggs</a> cannot be Recalled.
</li>
<li> <a href="/starcraft/Lurker_Egg" title="Lurker Egg" class="mw-redirect">Lurker Eggs</a> can not be Recalled, but <a href="/starcraft/Cocoon" title="Cocoon">Cocoons</a> can.
</li>
</ul>
