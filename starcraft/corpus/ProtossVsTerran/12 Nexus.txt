<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=12_Nexus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>12 Nexus</b> opening is a safer version of the <a href="/starcraft/14_Nexus" title="14 Nexus" class="mw-redirect">14 Nexus</a> Build Order, as it enables the Protoss to mass units earlier than its pendant.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Execution">Execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=12_Nexus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>12 Nexus</b> opening does not need much explanation, as it only determines the early game vs. Terran. Scouting however is pivotal, as the Protoss might need to adapt to aggressive counters. If Terran attempts to attack with the first Marines, a number of SCVs and a Bunker, the Zealot opening is recommended and Probes might be needed to defend. If the Terran opens with a very fast Expansion himself, the Zealot can be left out in order to gain a strong economic advantage. Since the Protoss will be able to build up a strong economy early on, delayed and safe strategies like a <a href="/starcraft/Siege_Expand" title="Siege Expand" class="mw-redirect">Siege Expand</a> are usually soft countered. However, the more popular <a href="/starcraft/14_CC_(vs._Protoss)" title="14 CC (vs. Protoss)">14 Command Center Build</a> puts the Protoss in a slight disadvantage, as both players will be even. An even economy usually supports Terran.
</p><p>The Protoss can transition into the mid game with any kind of strategy and defend two Factory pushes relatively easy, as he will be able to reinforce faster. If the Protoss faces a contain after a Two Factory opening, it is pivotal not to overreact. While a contain and Tanks in Siege Mode mean stress, it's a false positive. Terran needs to completely deny the expansion and will be on a very low economy himself. If Protoss is able to trade armies, he will come out on top. If the Terran uses a Vulture heavy 2 Factory opening, Pylons can be used to block the Choke point. 
</p><p>Against a non-aggressive Build the Protoss can chose to either transition to high Tech units like <a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Arbiters&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arbiters (page does not exist)">Arbiters</a> off two bases, or expand a third time if his army matches the strength of the Terran.
</p>
