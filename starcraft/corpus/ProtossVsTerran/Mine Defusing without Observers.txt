<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Clearing <a href="/starcraft/Spider_Mine" title="Spider Mine">Spider Mines</a> without an <a href="/starcraft/Observer" title="Observer">Observer</a> allows the Protoss player to be aggressive before <a href="/starcraft/Observer" title="Observer">Observers</a> are produced or in place. Defusing Mines without Observers is risky, and should used only when the benefits outweigh the risk. Generally, groups of <a href="/starcraft/Dragoon" title="Dragoon">Dragoons</a> are used for defusal, but units like weapons-upgraded <a href="/starcraft/Zealot" title="Zealot">Zealots</a> and <a href="/starcraft/Dark_Templar" title="Dark Templar">Dark Templar</a> can also defuse Mines.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Method">Method</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Method">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There several units that can be used to defuse Mines. Each unit requires different micro tactics.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dragoons">Dragoons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dragoons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The process for defusing Mines is as follows:
</p>
<ol>
<li>Click Forward to activate the Mine.
</li>
<li>Click Back.
</li>
<li>Hold position (press H) at about half the distance that you went forward (doesn't have to be where you clicked.) 
</li>
</ol>
<p>Alternative Method:
</p>
<ol>
<li>Click Forward to activate the Mine.
</li>
<li>Click Back.
</li>
<li>Press A and click <i>away from the Mine</i>. (Attack-Move backwards.)
</li>
</ol>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zealots">Zealots</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Zealots">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Zealots with a +2 Weapons Upgrade can kill a Spider Mine in one hit. If the Zealots do not have this upgrade, it is very risky to attempt Mine defusal as it will only be possible with multiple attackers attacking at once. If the player has not reached this upgrade, <a href="/starcraft/Mine_Dragging" title="Mine Dragging">Mine Dragging</a> is a better option in most cases so that one Zealot will absorb as many Mines as possible.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Templar">Dark Templar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dark Templar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Dark Templar possesses a 40 damage attack which can kill a Spider Mine in one hit. Ideally, if the Terran tries to defend by planting Mines, the Protoss player can snipe the Mines before they burrow. Careful micro allows Dark Templars to kill Mines that have already been laid, even if there is no Observer to support the Dark Templar.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Method_.28Not_Burrowed.29">Method (Not Burrowed)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Method (Not Burrowed)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Dark Templar will automatically target the Vulture as opposed to the Spider Mine, so you must perform basic micro.
</p>
<ul>
<li>Move the Dark Templar to where the Vulture is planting the Spider Mine.
</li>
<li>Right-click or Attack-Move click directly on the mine. It will take some time for the Spider Mine to burrow, then unburrow and attack. The goal is to kill the mine as quickly as possible.
</li>
<li>If the Vulture is not right next to the Dark Templar, but the Spider Mine is, Hold Position can also be used.
</li>
<li>As soon as the Spider Mine is killed, the Dark Templar should immediately attempt to defuse any other Mines being planted.
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Method_.28Already_Burrowed.29">Method (Already Burrowed)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/starcraft/index.php?title=Mine_Defusing_without_Observers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Method (Already Burrowed)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>If the Mine is already burrowed, defusing the Spider Mine becomes much more difficult. Although the Dark Templar can kill a Mine in one hit, there is a very small window in between the Mine unburrowing (and being attackable) and the Mine attacking. Therefore this should generally not be attempted when latency is a factor.
</p>
<ul>
<li>Tell the Dark Templar to move forward.
</li>
<li>Use Hold-Position when close to, but not touching the Mine. It is important that you are not directly next to the mine, or else it will explode instantly. Spider Mines have a short delay before they move in for the kill.
</li>
<li>If timed properly, the Dark Templar will kill the Mine before it attacks.
</li>
</ul>
